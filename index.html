The Basics

What Is Crawl Space Encapsulation?

Crawl space encapsulation is the process of sealing a vented crawl space with a heavy-duty vapor barrier, closing outside vents, and controlling humidity with a dehumidifier to create a clean, dry, conditioned space beneath a home. This permanent moisture-control solution prevents mold growth, wood rot, pest infestation, and the "stack effect" that draws damp, contaminated air up into living areas. The U.S. Department of Energy identifies sealed crawl spaces as a best practice for energy-efficient home construction in humid climates.

In the Roanoke Valley, where average annual humidity is 68% and rainfall exceeds 41 inches, an unsealed crawl space becomes a breeding ground for mold, wood-destroying fungi, and structural decay. Encapsulation is not an upgrade. It is essential protection for your home's foundation and indoor air quality.
