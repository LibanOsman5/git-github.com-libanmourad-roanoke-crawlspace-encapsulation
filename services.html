Crawl Space Services in Roanoke, VA

From a full encapsulation system to targeted moisture and mold control — everything your Roanoke Valley crawl space needs to stay dry, clean, and structurally sound.

🛡️

Crawl Space Encapsulation

A complete moisture-sealing system. We line the floor and walls with a heavy-duty reinforced vapor barrier, seal vents and penetrations, and add dehumidification to create a clean, dry, conditioned space. This is the permanent fix for humidity, musty air, and the mold and rot it causes. Most popular service for Roanoke homeowners.

💧

Vapor Barrier Installation

The foundation of any dry crawl space. Our reinforced liners stop ground moisture from wicking up into your subfloor, joists, and insulation — protecting framing and cutting the humidity that feeds mold. We use 12-mil or thicker material for long-term durability.

🌬️

Crawl Space Dehumidifiers

Virginia summers push humidity above 80%. We install energy-efficient crawl space dehumidifiers that keep relative humidity below 50% year-round, with automatic drainage that needs no emptying. Sized correctly for your crawl space dimensions.

🍄

Mold & Moisture Control

We treat existing mold growth with antimicrobial solutions, eliminate the moisture source driving it, and put a sealed system in place so it doesn't come back. The result: cleaner air upstairs and no more musty smell. Safe for your family and pets.

🔧

Crawl Space Repair

Sagging floors, rotted joists, fallen insulation, and failing supports — we restore the structural health of your crawl space before sealing it for the long term. Common in older Roanoke homes built before modern moisture standards.

🏠

Drainage & Sump Pump Systems

When water gets in, it needs somewhere to go. Interior drainage channels and sump pump systems keep standing water out of your encapsulated crawl space — essential for homes in low-lying areas of the Roanoke Valley.

Why a sealed crawl space matters in the Roanoke Valley

Roanoke's humid subtropical climate means your crawl space faces moisture 7+ months of the year. Here's why encapsulation isn't optional — it's essential:

  • Summer humidity in Roanoke averages 75–85% — vented crawl spaces trap that moisture, feeding mold and wood rot.
  • Up to 50% of your home's air originates below the floor — damp crawl space = damp indoor air you're breathing right now.
  • Sealing the space lowers HVAC strain by 15–20% (U.S. Department of Energy data).
  • Protects flooring, framing, and resale value — a dry crawl space is a selling point, a wet one is a liability.
